# Insulin Drug Interactions

## Drug Overview
- **Generic Name:** Insulin (human recombinant and analogs)
- **Drug Class:** Antidiabetic - Insulin
- **Primary Use:** Type 1 Diabetes, Type 2 Diabetes, Diabetic Ketoacidosis, Hyperkalemia
- **Mechanism:** Facilitates cellular glucose uptake by binding to insulin receptors; promotes glycogen synthesis; inhibits hepatic gluconeogenesis
- **Forms:** Rapid-acting, Short-acting, Intermediate-acting, Long-acting, Ultra-long-acting

---

## Major Drug-Drug Interactions (Severity: HIGH - Avoid or Use with Extreme Caution)

### 1. Pramlintide
- **Interacting Drug:** Pramlintide (Symlin)
- **Interaction Type:** Must be administered separately
- **Mechanism:** Both affect glucose regulation; pharmacokinetic incompatibility
- **Clinical Effect:** Severe hypoglycemia risk
- **Management:** Contraindicated to mix; administer separately; reduce insulin dose by 50% when initiating pramlintide
- **Source:** Medscape Drug Interactions; FDA Prescribing Information

### 2. Thiazolidinediones (High Dose)
- **Interacting Drugs:** Pioglitazone, Rosiglitazone
- **Interaction Type:** Pharmacodynamic synergism with fluid retention
- **Mechanism:** TZDs cause fluid retention; combined with insulin increases heart failure risk
- **Clinical Effect:** New or worsening heart failure; peripheral edema
- **Management:** Use with caution; monitor for signs of heart failure; may need dose reduction
- **Source:** FDA Prescribing Information; Drugs.com

### 3. Disopyramide
- **Interacting Drug:** Disopyramide (antiarrhythmic)
- **Interaction Type:** Additive hypoglycemic effect
- **Mechanism:** Disopyramide has independent hypoglycemic properties
- **Clinical Effect:** Severe hypoglycemia
- **Management:** Close glucose monitoring required; avoid if possible
- **Source:** Drugs.com Drug Interactions Database

### 4. Pentamidine
- **Interacting Drug:** Pentamidine
- **Interaction Type:** Biphasic glucose effect
- **Mechanism:** Initially causes hypoglycemia by beta-cell toxicity, then hyperglycemia from beta-cell destruction
- **Clinical Effect:** Unpredictable glucose swings; severe hypoglycemia followed by hyperglycemia
- **Management:** Close monitoring; may need insulin dose adjustments
- **Source:** FDA Prescribing Information (Lantus Label)

## Moderate Drug-Drug Interactions (Severity: MODERATE - Monitor Closely)

### Drugs That INCREASE Hypoglycemia Risk

#### Oral Antidiabetic Agents
- **Interacting Drugs:** 
  - Sulfonylureas: Glipizide, Glimepiride, Glyburide
  - Meglitinides: Repaglinide, Nateglinide
  - SGLT2 Inhibitors: Empagliflozin (Jardiance), Dapagliflozin, Canagliflozin
  - GLP-1 Agonists: Semaglutide (Ozempic), Dulaglutide, Liraglutide
  - DPP-4 Inhibitors: Sitagliptin (Januvia), Saxagliptin, Linagliptin
  - Metformin
- **Interaction Type:** Additive glucose lowering
- **Clinical Effect:** Increased hypoglycemia risk
- **Management:** Often used in combination; reduce insulin dose when adding other agents; monitor glucose closely
- **Source:** FDA Prescribing Information; Drugs.com

#### ACE Inhibitors
- **Interacting Drugs:** Lisinopril, Enalapril, Ramipril, Captopril, Benazepril, Fosinopril
- **Interaction Type:** Enhanced insulin sensitivity
- **Mechanism:** ACE inhibitors may improve insulin sensitivity
- **Clinical Effect:** Potential hypoglycemia
- **Management:** Monitor blood glucose; may need insulin dose reduction
- **Source:** Medscape Drug Interactions; Drugs.com

#### Angiotensin II Receptor Blockers (ARBs)
- **Interacting Drugs:** Losartan, Valsartan, Irbesartan, Candesartan, Olmesartan, Telmisartan
- **Interaction Type:** May enhance insulin effects
- **Mechanism:** Unspecified mechanism affecting glucose metabolism
- **Clinical Effect:** Possible hypoglycemia
- **Management:** Monitor glucose; adjust insulin as needed
- **Source:** Medscape Drug Interactions

#### Beta-Blockers
- **Interacting Drugs:** Propranolol, Metoprolol, Atenolol, Carvedilol, Bisoprolol, Labetalol
- **Interaction Type:** Dual effect - mask hypoglycemia symptoms AND may affect glucose
- **Mechanism:** Block adrenergic symptoms of hypoglycemia; some beta-blockers may prolong hypoglycemia
- **Clinical Effect:** Masked hypoglycemia symptoms (tachycardia, tremor); prolonged hypoglycemic episodes
- **Management:** Patient education on recognizing hypoglycemia; more frequent glucose monitoring
- **Source:** FDA Prescribing Information (Lantus); Drugs.com

#### Salicylates (High-Dose)
- **Interacting Drugs:** Aspirin (>3g/day), Salsalate
- **Interaction Type:** Enhanced glucose lowering
- **Mechanism:** Salicylates have intrinsic hypoglycemic properties
- **Clinical Effect:** Increased hypoglycemia risk
- **Management:** Monitor blood glucose with high-dose salicylate therapy
- **Source:** Drugs.com Drug Interactions Database

#### MAO Inhibitors
- **Interacting Drugs:** Phenelzine, Tranylcypromine, Isocarboxazid, Selegiline
- **Interaction Type:** Enhanced hypoglycemic effect
- **Mechanism:** MAOIs may increase insulin secretion and sensitivity
- **Clinical Effect:** Hypoglycemia
- **Management:** Close glucose monitoring; may need insulin dose reduction
- **Source:** FDA Prescribing Information; Drugs.com

#### Fluoxetine
- **Interacting Drug:** Fluoxetine (Prozac)
- **Interaction Type:** Enhanced insulin sensitivity
- **Mechanism:** Fluoxetine may improve insulin sensitivity
- **Clinical Effect:** Potential hypoglycemia
- **Management:** Monitor blood glucose
- **Source:** Drugs.com Drug Interactions Database

#### Fibrates
- **Interacting Drugs:** Fenofibrate, Gemfibrozil
- **Interaction Type:** May enhance insulin effects
- **Clinical Effect:** Possible hypoglycemia
- **Management:** Monitor blood glucose
- **Source:** Drugs.com

#### Guanethidine
- **Interacting Drug:** Guanethidine
- **Interaction Type:** Enhanced hypoglycemic effect
- **Mechanism:** May increase insulin sensitivity
- **Clinical Effect:** Hypoglycemia
- **Management:** Monitor closely
- **Source:** FDA Prescribing Information (Lantus)

### Drugs That DECREASE Insulin Efficacy (May Cause Hyperglycemia)

#### Corticosteroids
- **Interacting Drugs:** Prednisone, Prednisolone, Dexamethasone, Methylprednisolone, Hydrocortisone, Budesonide, Betamethasone
- **Interaction Type:** Pharmacodynamic antagonism
- **Mechanism:** Increase hepatic gluconeogenesis; cause insulin resistance
- **Clinical Effect:** Hyperglycemia; loss of glycemic control
- **Management:** May need significant insulin dose increases; monitor glucose closely
- **Source:** FDA Prescribing Information; Drugs.com

#### Atypical Antipsychotics
- **Interacting Drugs:** Olanzapine, Clozapine, Risperidone, Quetiapine, Aripiprazole, Ziprasidone
- **Interaction Type:** Metabolic effects causing insulin resistance
- **Mechanism:** Weight gain; direct effects on glucose metabolism
- **Clinical Effect:** Worsened glycemic control; hyperglycemia
- **Management:** Monitor glucose closely; may need insulin dose increase
- **Source:** Drugs.com; FDA Prescribing Information

#### Thyroid Hormones
- **Interacting Drugs:** Levothyroxine (Synthroid), Liothyronine
- **Interaction Type:** Pharmacodynamic antagonism
- **Mechanism:** Increase glucose absorption and hepatic glucose output
- **Clinical Effect:** May worsen glycemic control
- **Management:** Monitor glucose when initiating or adjusting thyroid medication
- **Source:** FDA Prescribing Information; Drugs.com

#### Diuretics
- **Interacting Drugs:** Furosemide (Lasix), Hydrochlorothiazide, Bumetanide, Torsemide
- **Interaction Type:** Pharmacodynamic antagonism
- **Mechanism:** May cause hyperglycemia; hypokalemia affects insulin secretion
- **Clinical Effect:** Decreased glucose control
- **Management:** Monitor blood glucose and potassium
- **Source:** FDA Prescribing Information; Drugs.com

#### Sympathomimetics
- **Interacting Drugs:** Epinephrine, Albuterol, Terbutaline, Pseudoephedrine, Phenylephrine
- **Interaction Type:** Counter-regulatory hormone effect
- **Mechanism:** Stimulate glycogenolysis and gluconeogenesis
- **Clinical Effect:** Hyperglycemia
- **Management:** May need temporary insulin dose increase during acute use
- **Source:** FDA Prescribing Information; Drugs.com

#### Estrogens and Oral Contraceptives
- **Interacting Drugs:** Ethinyl estradiol, Combined oral contraceptives
- **Interaction Type:** Pharmacodynamic antagonism
- **Mechanism:** May impair glucose tolerance
- **Clinical Effect:** Possible decrease in glycemic control
- **Management:** Monitor blood glucose
- **Source:** FDA Prescribing Information; Medscape

#### Protease Inhibitors
- **Interacting Drugs:** Ritonavir, Atazanavir, Darunavir, Lopinavir, Nelfinavir, Saquinavir
- **Interaction Type:** Metabolic effects
- **Mechanism:** May cause insulin resistance and hyperglycemia
- **Clinical Effect:** New-onset diabetes or worsened control
- **Management:** Monitor glucose closely; adjust insulin as needed
- **Source:** Drugs.com Drug Interactions Database

#### Danazol
- **Interacting Drug:** Danazol
- **Interaction Type:** Hyperglycemic effect
- **Mechanism:** Causes insulin resistance
- **Clinical Effect:** Worsened glycemic control
- **Management:** Monitor glucose; increase insulin as needed
- **Source:** Drugs.com

#### Phenytoin
- **Interacting Drug:** Phenytoin
- **Interaction Type:** Inhibits insulin secretion
- **Mechanism:** Direct effect on pancreatic beta cells
- **Clinical Effect:** Hyperglycemia
- **Management:** Monitor glucose closely
- **Source:** FDA Prescribing Information

#### Nicotinic Acid (Niacin)
- **Interacting Drug:** Niacin
- **Interaction Type:** Impairs glucose tolerance
- **Mechanism:** Affects insulin sensitivity
- **Clinical Effect:** Hyperglycemia
- **Management:** Monitor glucose; adjust insulin as needed
- **Source:** FDA Prescribing Information

## Drug-Food Interactions

### Alcohol
- **Severity:** Moderate
- **Mechanism:** Alcohol inhibits hepatic gluconeogenesis; can cause both hypoglycemia and hyperglycemia
- **Clinical Effect:** 
  - Hypoglycemia (especially when fasting or with delayed meals)
  - Hyperglycemia (with excessive intake adding calories)
- **Recommendation:** Moderate consumption only if diabetes well-controlled; avoid drinking on empty stomach
- **Source:** FDA Prescribing Information; Drugs.com

### Meals and Timing
- **Rapid-acting insulins:** Must be taken with meals
- **Long-acting insulins:** Can be taken regardless of meals
- **Food Effect:** Delays gastric emptying may affect insulin timing with rapid-acting formulations
- **Source:** FDA Prescribing Information

## Disease Interactions

### 1. Renal Impairment (Moderate)
- **Effect:** Reduced insulin clearance
- **Risk:** Prolonged hypoglycemia
- **Management:** May need dose reduction; careful monitoring
- **Source:** FDA Prescribing Information; Drugs.com

### 2. Hepatic Impairment (Moderate)
- **Effect:** Reduced gluconeogenesis capacity; decreased insulin metabolism
- **Risk:** Increased hypoglycemia risk
- **Management:** May need dose reduction; careful monitoring
- **Source:** FDA Prescribing Information; Drugs.com

### 3. Hypokalemia (Moderate)
- **Effect:** Insulin shifts potassium intracellularly
- **Risk:** Worsening hypokalemia; cardiac arrhythmias
- **Management:** Monitor potassium levels; correct hypokalemia
- **Caution:** Especially with concurrent loop diuretics, thiazides
- **Source:** FDA Prescribing Information; Drugs.com

### 4. Hypoglycemia (Moderate)
- **At-Risk Patients:**
  - Elderly patients
  - Patients with autonomic neuropathy
  - Those with irregular eating patterns
  - Those taking beta-blockers
  - Patients with renal/hepatic impairment
- **Management:** Individualized dosing; patient education; glucose monitoring
- **Source:** FDA Prescribing Information; Drugs.com

## Clinical Recommendations for Safe Use

1. **Patient Selection:**
   - Review complete medication list
   - Assess renal and hepatic function
   - Identify patients at high hypoglycemia risk

2. **Monitoring:**
   - Regular blood glucose monitoring (SMBG)
   - Periodic HbA1c measurements
   - Monitor for hypoglycemia symptoms
   - Potassium monitoring with diuretics

3. **Dose Adjustments:**
   - Reduce insulin when adding other glucose-lowering agents
   - Increase insulin during steroid therapy
   - Adjust for renal/hepatic impairment

4. **Patient Education:**
   - Recognize hypoglycemia symptoms (especially with beta-blockers)
   - Proper injection technique
   - Meal timing with rapid-acting insulins
   - Sick day management
   - Avoid sharing insulin pens/syringes
